Every developer eventually writes a "Hello World" post. Today is mine.

## Why now?

I've been meaning to start a blog for *years*. I'd open a notes app, type a few paragraphs, then close it thinking "who would read this?" 

The answer I finally landed on: **it doesn't matter**. Writing is thinking made visible. Even if no one reads this, the act of writing forces me to slow down, clarify my thoughts, and notice things I'd otherwise scroll past.

## What this blog is for

No strict theme. No content calendar. Just honest writing about:

- **Tech & Coding** — things I'm building, learning, or wrestling with
- **Design** — because I think about how things look and feel constantly  
- **Career** — lessons from working in tech, navigating the industry
- **Personal life** — because we're all more than our job titles

## Who I'm writing for

Honestly? Mostly for myself. But if something I write saves you an hour of debugging, makes you laugh, or just makes you feel less alone in the chaos of life and code — that's a wonderful bonus.
